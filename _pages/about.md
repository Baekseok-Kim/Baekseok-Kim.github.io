I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the [University of Nevada, Las Vegas (UNLV)](https://www.unlv.edu/), advised by [Prof. Paul Y. Oh](https://www.unlv.edu/people/paul-oh). I work in the Drones and Autonomous Systems Laboratory (DASL), where my research focuses on **humanoid locomotion**, **loco-manipulation**, and **learning-based whole-body control**.

Research Interests
======
- Bipedal locomotion and loco-manipulation for humanoid robots
- Reinforcement learning and hybrid model-based / learning-based control
- Telepresence avatar systems and human–robot collaboration in hazardous environments
- Mechatronic system design, from hardware platforms to real-time control software

Current Work
======
My doctoral research centers on building practical humanoid platforms and control architectures that remain stable while walking and manipulating under external interaction forces. Recent projects include hierarchical loco-manipulation with pre-IK residual RL, force-augmented LIPM–MPC, and the Stilt-bot—a simplified underactuated biped for AI-ready walking control. I lead system integration across mechanical design, simulation, and onboard control for DASL's bipedal research platforms.

Before UNLV, I earned an M.S. in Interdisciplinary Robot Engineering Systems from Hanyang University, where I developed simulation and control tools for a skiing humanoid robot. I also spent three years as a robotics engineer at Global Zeus (ZEUS Co., Ltd.) in South Korea, leading the development of parallel and SCARA industrial robots and their motion-control systems.
